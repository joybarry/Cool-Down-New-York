CASE STUDY: Exploring a Particular Area Using DATA2GO.NYC: Morris Heights, Fordham South, and Mount Hope

This district has the highest percentage of families who live below the poverty line.

This district (and its surrounding area) produces relatively little waste per person compared to areas with less trash burden.

However, the risk of health issues that can be traced back to pollution and inadequate resources is relatively high in that same district.

All of this combined indicates that there is toxic waste harming Morris Heights, Fordham South, and Mount Hope, but this waste is not a product of the people who actually live in the area. DATA2GO.NYC New York City’s “overburdened districts”, according to the Waste Equity Law, are the South Bronx, North Brooklyn, and Southeast Queens.

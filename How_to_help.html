Some concerns that explain the resistance to this bill are that jobs will disappear and consumers will lose their freedom to choose their energy source. New Yorkers can stay up to date with clean energy initiatives by contacting the New York Power Authority for information on current progress and getting involved in community training through the Office of Just Energy Transition and specialized programs.

Participate in resilience plans designed by local community leaders or organize your own efforts to help your area adapt quickly to environmental stress. Conservation of ecosystems and urban areas are both essential to our defense against climate change. The best way to ensure that your work is worthwhile is to stay involved and informed about the short and long term impacts of the implemented adaptations. Visit What New Yorkers Can Do: Mitigation, Adaptation, And Resilience - NYSDEC for more information!
